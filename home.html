Brief Overview on Poverty

It is about not having enough money to meet basic needs including food, clothing and shelter. Poverty entails more than the lack of income and productive resources to ensure sustainable livelihoods. Its manifestations include hunger and malnutrition, limited access to education and other basic services, social discrimination and exclusion as well as the lack of participation in decision-making. Lack of access to basic services, education, employment opportunities, as well as climate change all serve to perpetuate poverty.


Poverty in the Philippines

In the Philippines, 18.1% of the population lived below the national poverty line in 2021. In the Philippines, the proportion of employed population below $2.15 purchasing power parity a day in 2023 was 0.2%. For every 1,000 babies born in the Philippines in 2022, 27 died before their 5th birthday. overty in the Philippines has been linked to bad governance, corruption, and a political system dominated by political dynasties. The country's poorest provinces are ruled by political dynasties. Natural disasters have exacerbated poverty in the Philippines.

Poverty in the Philippines is a serious issue caused by many factors. A large number of people live below the poverty line, especially in rural areas where jobs and resources are hard to find. Many work in low-paying, unstable jobs, while barriers to education, like high dropout rates and poor school facilities, make it difficult for children to succeed. Access to healthcare is also limited, leading to preventable diseases and malnutrition. Natural disasters, which happen often, disrupt lives and make recovery challenging, trapping families in poverty. Despite some government efforts to help, problems like corruption and poor implementation make it hard to see real progress. To tackle poverty effectively, a combination of strategies focused on economic growth, education, and disaster resilience is needed.
